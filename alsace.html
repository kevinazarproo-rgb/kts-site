Alsace, between vineyards and storybook villages

From Strasbourg to Colmar, along the Wine Route, Alsace blends French and Germanic influences: half-timbered houses, grands crus, hearty gastronomy and, in winter, the most famous Christmas markets in France.

Cities & places

Along the Wine Route

Strasbourg

The Gothic cathedral and its astronomical clock, the canals of La Petite France and the European Parliament — a city both historic and cosmopolitan.

Colmar

Flower-lined canals and pastel half-timbered houses earn its old town the nickname “Little Venice” — the most picturesque town in Alsace.

Riquewihr

A medieval walled village surrounded by grand cru vineyards, among the most beautiful villages in France.

Route des Vins

170 km from Marlenheim to Thann, lined with wine villages, family estates and tasting cellars.

Haut-Koenigsbourg

A restored medieval fortress perched at 800 m, with sweeping views over the Alsace plain and the Black Forest.

Eguisheim

The birthplace of Alsace wine, built in concentric circles around its castle — flowered, intimate and timeless.

Identity

At the crossroads of France and Germany

Few regions carry such a singular identity. For centuries, Alsace passed between France and Germany, and from this history it has kept a unique blend: half-timbered houses and flower-filled balconies, a Germanic dialect and French art de vivre, Christmas traditions and Michelin gastronomy.

It is this dual culture that gives Alsace its fairy-tale character — and makes every village, every winstub and every market feel like nowhere else in France.

Experiences

The Alsatian art of living

Vineyards

The Wine Route & grands crus

We arrange private tastings with the winemakers, cellar visits and lunches among the vines along one of France's oldest wine routes. The 51 Alsace grands crus — Riesling, Gewurztraminer, Pinot Gris — open their doors to you.

Villages

Storybook villages & Christmas markets

Colmar, Riquewihr and Eguisheim look straight out of a fairy tale, especially in December when the illuminated Christmas markets light up the squares. We arrange private strolls and after-hours access, away from the crowds.

Art of living

Alsatian gastronomy

From the friendly winstub to the Michelin-starred table, Alsace is a land of generous cuisine — choucroute, tarte flambée, foie gras — paired with its great white wines.
