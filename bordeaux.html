Bordeaux, the world capital of wine

A UNESCO World Heritage city, world-renowned vineyards and the Atlantic nearby: Bordeaux pairs 18th-century elegance, gastronomy and the most prestigious wine châteaux in the world.

Cities & places

Bordeaux & its vineyards

Place de la Bourse

Its famous water mirror, the stone quays along the Garonne and the largest 18th-century urban ensemble in France — the elegant heart of a city listed by UNESCO.

Saint-Émilion

A medieval wine village listed by UNESCO, ringed by grands crus and famous for its monolithic church carved straight into the rock.

Médoc

Along the estuary, the legendary classified-growth châteaux of Pauillac, Saint-Julien and Saint-Estèphe — home to some of the greatest red wines on earth.

Margaux

One of the most prestigious appellations of the Médoc, where elegant estates produce red wines of rare finesse.

Pessac-Léognan

At the gates of the city, the great reds and whites of the Graves, including the legendary Château Haut-Brion.

Arcachon

Oysters, the bay and the Dune du Pilat — Europe’s tallest sand dune — an hour from the vines, where Bordeaux meets the Atlantic.

The Atlantic

Arcachon & the Dune du Pilat

An hour from the vineyards, Bordeaux opens onto the ocean. We arrange an oyster tasting on the Arcachon bay, straight from the producer, and the climb up the Dune du Pilat — Europe’s tallest — for sunset over the Atlantic and the endless pine forest.

Heritage

The world capital of wine

For centuries, Bordeaux has built its fortune and its elegance on the vine. Around the city stretch some of the most prestigious vineyards on the planet — the Médoc, Saint-Émilion, Pomerol, the Graves and Sauternes — whose names alone evoke the very greatest wines.

From the 1855 classification to today’s grands crus, wine here is a culture, an art and a way of life. KTS opens the doors of the great châteaux that usually remain closed to the public.

When to go

Each season, a different Bordeaux

  • Spring · April–JuneThe vineyards turn green, the weather is mild and the city comes alive before the summer.
  • Summer · July–AugustThe Atlantic nearby — oysters on the Arcachon bay and sunset over the Dune du Pilat.
  • Autumn · September–OctoberThe grape harvest, golden vines and the great wine season, the most beautiful time in the châteaux.
