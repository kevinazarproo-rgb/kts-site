Burgundy

Burgundy, land of great wines

From the vineyards of the Côte de Nuits to the Hospices de Beaune, from the abbeys of Cluny and Vézelay to the canals of the Morvan, Burgundy unfolds an art of living made of grands crus, golden stone and generous tables — in the very heart of France.

Identity

Where the climats were born

From the Dukes of Burgundy to the abbeys of Cluny and Cîteaux, the region shaped Europe. Its “climats” — the named plots of the Côte — are today listed as UNESCO World Heritage.

Bœuf bourguignon and escargots, Chardonnay and Pinot Noir, stone villages and Romanesque churches — Burgundy blends a winegrower’s soul with a true French art of living.
