Corsica, the Island of Beauty

Just off the French Riviera, Corsica rises from the Mediterranean like a mountain in the sea. On a single island: white-sand beaches and turquoise coves, peaks over 2,000 metres, hilltop villages and one of the strongest identities in France.

Island

Across the island

Ajaccio

The capital, Napoleon’s birthplace, its gulf and the Sanguinaires islands.

Bonifacio

A citadel perched on white limestone cliffs above the sea, at the island’s southern tip.

Calvi

Its Genoese citadel, a long bay and pine-backed beaches, in the Balagne.

Porto-Vecchio

Gateway to the south and its famous beaches, Palombaggia and Santa Giulia.

Scandola & Piana

A UNESCO marine reserve and the red porphyry calanques, reached by boat.

Bavella & the GR20

Jagged peaks and the legendary trail, the wild heart of the island.

Signature

Where the mountains meet the sea

Nowhere else in the Mediterranean do 2,000-metre peaks rise so close to the water. In the morning, a turquoise cove; in the afternoon, a forest of Laricio pines or a mountain pass. We combine the two faces of the island — coast and summits — and reach the most secret coves by private boat.

Identity

An island with a soul

Corsica has its own language, its own songs and a fierce attachment to its land. From the scent of the maquis to the village feast days, the island lives at its own rhythm, proud and warm.

French and Genoese heritage, a cuisine of charcuterie, brocciu cheese and chestnut, and wines found nowhere else — Corsica is a destination with a character all its own.

When to go

Each season, a different Corsica

  • May–JuneMild weather, the maquis in bloom and the sea warming — before the summer crowds.
  • July–AugustHot and lively, the beaches at their best — book well ahead.
  • September–OctoberA warm sea, quieter coves and ideal hiking weather.
