The Alps

Mont-Blanc, the roof of Europe

Chamonix sits at the foot of Mont-Blanc — at 4,808 metres the highest peak in the Alps and the birthplace of mountaineering. All around, France’s most prestigious resorts — Megève, Courchevel, Val d’Isère — combine world-class slopes with grand chalets, spas and Michelin tables.

Identity

Where alpinism was born

Chamonix is where modern mountaineering began, with the first ascent of Mont-Blanc in 1786, and where the very first Winter Olympics were held in 1924. The valley still lives to the rhythm of the high mountains, guides and the great north faces.

Grand hotels and family chalets, fondue and génépi, ski in winter and trails in summer — the Alps blend sporting spirit with a true French art of living.
