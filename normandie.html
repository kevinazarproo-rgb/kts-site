Normandy

Normandy, history and the sea

From the abbey of Mont-Saint-Michel to the cliffs of Étretat, from the D-Day beaches to the harbours that gave birth to Impressionism, Normandy unfolds a coastline of memory and light, all within easy reach of Paris.

The coast

Around Normandy

Mont-Saint-Michel

The abbey island rising from its vast bay, a marvel of the medieval West.

Étretat

Dramatic white chalk cliffs and natural arches above the Channel.

Honfleur

A picturesque harbour of slate-fronted houses, cradle of Impressionism.

Deauville

The elegant seaside resort, with its boardwalk, villas and racecourse.

D-Day Landing Beaches

Omaha, Utah and the landing beaches, places of memory of June 1944.

Rouen

A city of a hundred spires, its gothic cathedral and half-timbered streets.

The seaside manor

Normandy at its best is a private manor or grand villa of your own by the sea: a dedicated chef, calvados by the fire, oysters fresh from the morning market. Identity

Where history meets the sea

From William the Conqueror to the June 1944 landings, Normandy has shaped the history of Europe. Its bocage, its abbeys and its beaches carry the memory of a thousand years.

Cider and Camembert, oysters and grey shrimp, the changing skies that inspired Monet, Normandy blends a maritime soul with a true French art of living.

When to go

Each season, a different coast

  • April–JuneSpring light, blossoming orchards and quiet beaches.
  • July–AugustThe seaside in full swing, festivals and long luminous evenings.
  • September–OctoberSoft light, the cider harvest and golden coastal walks.
