Île-de-France

Paris, capital of emotions

From dawn on the Seine to the gilt of the Opéra, Paris unfolds as a succession of rare moments. KTS composes your stay around privileged access, confidential tables and places one does not merely visit — one inhabits.

Cities & places

Paris and its region

Versailles

The most sumptuous of châteaux: the Hall of Mirrors, the State Apartments and gardens by Le Nôtre. Private visits before opening, dinners in the royal salons and Grandes Eaux evenings on request.

Giverny

Claude Monet's house and gardens, cradle of Impressionism. The water-lily pond and the Japanese bridge, discovered away from the crowds at first light with a private art guide.

Chantilly

A princely château housing the Condé Museum, its Great Stables and a French formal park. Fine dining, a tasting of Chantilly cream and a private equestrian show.

Fontainebleau

From François I to Napoleon, a royal residence nestled in a legendary forest. A thousand years of French history in an intimate setting, far from the crowds.

Reims

An hour from Paris, the cathedral of the kings' coronations and the gateway to Champagne. Prestigious cellars and private tastings at the greatest houses.

Vaux-le-Vicomte

The masterpiece that inspired Versailles. Candlelit evenings in Le Nôtre's gardens and private dinners beneath the gilding of the Grand Salon.
