Indian Ocean

Seychelles, the Indian Ocean at its purest

115 granite and coral islands scattered across the Indian Ocean, two hours from the equator. The Seychelles combine powder-white beaches, warm waters all year round, protected nature and some of the most exclusive private-island resorts in the world.

Islands

Islands of the archipelago

Mahé

The main island and its capital Victoria — the smallest in the world. Beau Vallon beach, the Morne Seychellois National Park and the international gateway.

Praslin

Home to Anse Lazio, regularly ranked among the world’s finest beaches, and to the Vallée de Mai.

La Digue

A car-free island explored by bike or ox-cart, and Anse Source d’Argent with its sculpted granite boulders.

Vallée de Mai

A UNESCO-listed primeval forest, the only home of the coco de mer — the largest seed in the plant kingdom.

Sainte Anne Marine Park

Six islands off Mahé, a protected marine park for snorkelling among reefs, rays and turtles.

Private islands

Entire private islands, given over to a handful of villas with a private beach and dedicated staff — for total seclusion.

Signature

Private islands & barefoot luxury

The Seychelles are home to some of the most exclusive resorts on the planet. Entire islands — North Island, Frégate, Six Senses Zil Pasyon — given over to a handful of villas, with a private beach, a butler and a chef. We arrange the stay, the transfers by helicopter or private boat, and every detail of your seclusion.

Identity

An archipelago like no other

The Seychelles are unlike any other island destination. They are the oldest oceanic islands on earth, with granite peaks, a unique flora — the coco de mer, found nowhere else — and giant tortoises.

Half the land is protected as nature reserve, and the sea is just as preserved. It is this rare nature, combined with discreet, high-end hospitality, that makes the archipelago a sanctuary for those who want beauty without the crowds.

When to go

Each season, a different Seychelles

  • April–May & Oct–NovemberCalm seas and clear water: the best windows for diving, between the two trade winds.
  • May–SeptemberDrier and a little cooler, the southeast trade wind: ideal for sailing.
  • November–MarchWarm and lush, the northwest wind: nature at its greenest, fewer travellers.
