Châteaux

Loire Valley, the garden of kings

Less than two hours from Paris, the Loire Valley lines up the most beautiful Renaissance châteaux in France along a UNESCO-listed river. KTS composes princely stays here, between châteaux, gardens, vineyards and the gentle art of living.

Cities & places

Along the châteaux

Chambord

The largest château of the Loire, a Renaissance masterpiece with its double-helix staircase attributed to Leonardo da Vinci, set in a vast walled estate.

Chenonceau

The Ladies' château, gracefully spanning the river Cher — the most visited and most romantic of the Loire.

Villandry

Famous for its extraordinary Renaissance gardens, among the most beautiful in France.

Amboise

A royal residence overlooking the river and the final home of Leonardo da Vinci, at the Clos Lucé.

Saumur

The Cadre noir, sparkling wines and troglodyte cellars carved into the tuffeau stone.

Tours

The elegant, gourmet capital of the valley, with its old town and lively squares — a perfect base.

Heritage

In the footsteps of Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci spent his final three years at the Clos Lucé in Amboise, as a guest of François I. We arrange a private visit of his manor and its gardens, where his machines come to life — the last home of a Renaissance genius, a few steps from the royal château.

A princely stay

Châteaux

Châteaux & privatisations

We arrange private visits of the finest châteaux — Chambord, Chenonceau, Cheverny — before opening or after closing, away from the crowds. With expert guides, you enter rooms usually closed to the public. KTS can also privatise a château for a dinner or an event in the royal salons.

Nature

Gardens & river

The Loire is the last wild river in Europe, best discovered on the water aboard a traditional flat-bottomed boat. On land, we open the gardens of Villandry at first light and plan cycling routes (the Loire à Vélo) between châteaux and vineyards. A hot-air balloon flight offers the finest view over the valley.

Gastronomy

Wines & château tables

The Loire is one of France's great wine regions: Vouvray, Sancerre, Chinon and Saumur. We organise tastings with the winemakers, often in cellars carved into the tuffeau stone. Evenings continue with gourmet dinners in historic residences and Michelin-starred tables.

Heritage

The cradle of the French Renaissance

It was in the Loire Valley that the French Renaissance took root. From François I to Catherine de Médicis, the kings of France made it their playground, raising châteaux of light and inviting the greatest artists of their time.

From Chambord’s double-helix staircase to the formal gardens of Villandry, this royal taste for beauty shaped an entire landscape — a concentration of art, architecture and gardens unmatched anywhere in France.
